APDMCx (x=1 or 2) APM None This is a WAP display. When an upper or lower Angle of Attack (AOA) vane is at max deflection (55-60 degrees) and the opposite side upper or lower AOA vane is less than 0 degree, one or both APDMCs will shut down when the FCCs are off, in MBIT, or in PFBIT. This is MCD (DU) Avionics Fault List display caused by Non-Volatile Random Access Memory (NOVRAM) corruption. NOVRAM corruptions do not inhibit normal APDMC operation. The corruption is defined as undesired data in any of the 32,000+ memory locations. The APDMC NOVRAM fault signs are that APM1 or APM2 is active on the Avionics Fault List and will remain after a circuit breaker reset is performed and the IBIT fails. It is permissible to fly the aircraft with this fault message when conditions in the Action Required column are met. When Flight Control Computer (FCC) aborted out of MBIT or PFBIT and APDMC1 or 2 is displayed on the WAP, reset the failed APDMC circuit breaker, initiate STALL TEST by turning STALL TEST switch to 1 and 2. Re-run FCC PFBIT or MBIT. Observe WAP: APDMCx is not displayed on the WAP. Verify a NOVRAM corruption has occurred with the following conditions: 1. The affected APDMC is on the Avionics Fault List, APMx. 2. No APDMC WAP message. 3. The APDMC fault does not clear with a circuit breaker reset. 4. The APDMC fails IBIT. 5. Review MCD (DU) Avionics Status Page (LRU10 for APM1 and LRU58 for APM2) status words and subaddress 20 message for the following: (a) The status word is 0020 0000 5000 0000 (b) The second word in the Subaddress 20 message at the bottom of the status page is 8000. (b) The second word in the Subaddress 20 message at the bottom of the status page is 8000.

The nominal rate for the PTA is 0.15 degrees of stab/second, with one motor driving. The time it takes the PTA to achieve 0.091 degrees of stab/second increases following extended periods of flight at cold temperature. Perform Horizontal Stabilizer System Operational Checkout (27-40-01) and Horizontal Stabilizer Position Sensor Operational Checkout (27-45-11). Return aircraft to service when no problems are detected. When problems are detected, refer to fault codes discovered during operational checkouts.

THRST REV x DISAG (x=1, 2, 3, or 4) The THRST REV x DISAG Master Caution Warning can occur while starting an engine with the throttle initially set to move the thrust reverser, especially on aircraft equipped with EEC Back-Up Power. EEC logic senses that reverser position disagrees with commanded position and logs the REVHYD fault before N2 rises above 10.5% rpm, and the EEC can power the thrust reverser control valve solenoids. When engine acceleration is fast enough or the reverser translates slowly enough, THRST REV x DISAG will be annunciated when the fuel switch is turned ON for the start. Verify THRST REV x DISAG clears shortly after the FUEL switch for the engine was place to ON during the start procedure. During battery start procedures , when the hydraulic pressure was not available during the start, verify THRST REV xDISAG clears shortly after hydraulic pressure is applied.
