Mission Essential List

MESL. Table Definitions/Column Identifiers. MESL tables are arranged by REFDES to provide Maintenance Supervision a mechanism to determine minimum system requirements and aircraft statusing. Components are listed by number Installed and minimum required for flight. Requirements are defined by Home Station Departure (HSD)/Main Operating Base (MOB) and En-route departures (ERD). Local training missions, to include off-station trainers, fall under ERD. An asterisk (*) in the Required column indicates the number required is situation dependent; refer to the notes column for clarification.
- The following locations are MOBs for AMC and AMC-gained aircrews: Joint Base Charleston, Dover AFB, Joint Base Lewis-McChord AFB, Joint Base McGuire-Dix-Lakehurst, and Travis AFB.
- PACAF and PACAF-gained crews will consider AMC MOBs as well as Joint Base Elmendorf-Richardson, AK and Joint Base Pearl Harbor-Hickam, HI as MOBs.
- ARC crews will consider AMC locations and MOBs, in addition to March ARB (AFRC), Wright Patterson ARB (AFRC), Pittsburgh IAP, PA (AFRC), Jackson, MS, (ANG), Martinsburg, WV (ANG), Memphis, TN (ANG), Stewart, NY (ANG), and Charlotte, NC (ANG) As MOBS.
- AETC will consider Altus AFB a MOB.

- When transiting a MOB on de-positioning leg use ERD Column. Example: A McChord C-17 transiting Charleston enroute to Ramstein AB (active) will use HSD Column. However, when transiting Charleston enroute to McChord (de-positioning) use ERD Column. Note: ERD Column requirements will not normally be waived when transiting a MOB on de-positioning leg. Crews transiting other component MOBs will consider those locations as an enroute support. Example: An AMC crew transiting Joint Base Elmendorf-Richardson, AK or Joint Base Pearl Harbor-Hickam, HI will use ERD Column for both active and de-positioning legs.

Mission Types - Air Cargo Drop (ACD); Air Drop Cargo/Personnel (ADCP); Air Drop Personnel (ADP); Air Refuel Conventional (ARC); Air Med Evac (AME) Status aircraft by using the FSL and BSL columns IAW AFI 21-103. HSD and ERD are AFI 21-103 identified mission types. Therefore these columns are to be considered BSL items for statusing consideration.

Shall be repaired at Repair Capable Facility (RCF) Stations with the necessary skilled maintenance personnel, support equipment, and facilities to accomplish required repairs. In those instances where lack of aforementioned requirements would cause a mission delay, the enroute location can be considered non-repair capable and aircraft may continue operations as described in the MESL.
